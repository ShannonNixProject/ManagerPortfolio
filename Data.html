Ensuring Data Accuracy in Device ID Marketing Project

Step-by-Step Process Using the Project Management Lifecycle

1. Initiation (Identified the Problem & Defined the Business Case)

  • Problem Statement: Device ID marketing campaigns relied on third-party data, but inaccuracies led to wasted ad spend and ineffective targeting.
  • Business Need: Improve data validation and ensure marketing campaigns are driven by accurate, high-quality data.
  • Key Challenge: Discrepancies between agency reports and internal records caused confusion in campaign performance analysis.
  • Stakeholder Alignment: Engaged marketing, sales, and analytics teams to define data integrity objectives.
  • Approval & Buy-In: Leadership approved a **4-month Lean Six Sigma project** to enhance data accuracy.

2. Planning (Developed the Data Accuracy Strategy)

  • Project Roadmap: Created a structured **4-month Lean Six Sigma implementation plan**.
  • Scope Definition: Focused on identifying root causes, improving validation processes, and ensuring consistent data reporting.
  • Risk Assessment: Planned mitigation strategies for data mismatches, reporting delays, and integration issues.
  • Training Plan:
    • Developed **data accuracy training sessions** for marketing and analytics teams.
    • Created onboarding materials, including process guides and validation checklists.
    • Planned interactive workshops on **data reconciliation and error identification**.
  • Resource Allocation: Assigned analytics, IT, and marketing teams to key roles.
  • Project Timeline:
    • Month 1: Data audit and initial validation process setup.
    • Month 2: Automation implementation and staff training.
    • Month 3: Testing, process refinement, and integration validation.
    • Month 4: Full deployment and ongoing monitoring.

3. Execution (Implementing Data Reconciliation & Validation)

  • Data Audit: Conducted a deep dive into third-party data sources to detect inconsistencies.
  • Validation Implementation: Established **standardized data validation** processes before launching campaigns.
  • Automated Data Reconciliation: Developed an internal system to compare agency-provided data with internal tracking records.
  • Training Employees & Stakeholders:
    • Held **hands-on training sessions** on data accuracy best practices.
    • Provided live demonstrations of automated reconciliation tools.
    • Developed role-specific training modules for analysts, marketers, and sales teams.

4. Monitoring & Controlling (Testing, Optimization & Risk Management)

  • Issue Resolution: Identified and corrected data discrepancies before campaign execution.
  • Performance Tracking: Measured improvements in targeting accuracy and engagement rates.
  • Ongoing Training & Support:
    • Collected feedback from teams to refine training materials.
    • Provided **follow-up workshops on troubleshooting data issues**.
    • Implemented a **dedicated data validation help desk**.
